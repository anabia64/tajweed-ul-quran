Noon Saakinah and Tanween

Similar rules are applied for Noon Saakinah and Tanween since they sound the same. There are three rules depending on the letter coming after Noon Saakinah or Tanween

Rules of Noon Saakinah and Tanween

Idhaar

This means “to make clear”.
This rule is applied when after Noon Saakin or Tanween there appear any of the Harooful Haalqiyya (throat letters), then they will be pronounced clearly.
No prolongation or the nasalization of the sound on Noon or Tanween takes place

Idghaam

Idghaam or assimilation occurs if after Noon Saakin or Tanween there appear any of the following letters

The above letters will have a shadd sign that will indicate that idghaam is to be applied.

Two Types of Idghaam

Idghaam Bila Ghunna (Idghaam without Ghunna)

On the following letters, we apply idghaam bila ghunna (idghaam without ghunna)

The sound of the Noon disappears completely as the word is merged with the following Laam or Raa.

Idghaam Ma’al Ghunna (Idghaam with Ghunna)

On the following letters, we apply Idghaam Ma’al Ghunna (Idghaam with Ghunna)

As the two words are merged, the sound of the Noon will not completely disappear.
Instead it will be read with a nasalized, prolonged sound (not more than than two Harakas) .

Ikhfaa

Ikhfaa literally means “to hide”
If any of the letters other than the Throat letters ( ه۶ غ ع خ ح ) or Idghaam letters ( ن و ل م ر ی ) come after Noon Saakin or Tanween the word must be read with Ghunna or nasalization. The length of the Ghunna will be equal to the duration of two harkas.
Ikhfaa is applied on the following 15 letters.

Iqlaab

When the letter ( ب ) comes after Noon Sakinah or tanween then the sound of the Noon Sakinah will be converted to ( م )
The harakah of the letter ( ب ) does not matter.
We usually see a small letter ( م ) on top of the ( ن ) when this rule is to be applied, in most scripts of the Quran.
Furthermore, this ( م ) is not read clearly like a normal ( م ). The sound of the ( م ) is hidden and prolonged.
