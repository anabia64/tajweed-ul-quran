Maddul Asli or original elongation

Letters with Harakah are prolonged if they are followed by their corresponding Harooful Madd ( ا ی و). Such type of elongation is called Maddul Asli or original elongation.
Exception: If Fatha is followed by its corresponding Harful Madd i.e Alif ( ا) with a sukoon, then it is not prolonged but rather recited with a jerk

Maddul Muttasil or Joined Madd

If Maddul Asli is followed by a Hamza (ء) in the same word, the elongation of Harooful Madd is increased to upto 4 to 6 Harakah. Such Madd is called Maddul Muttasil or Joined Madd.

Maddul Munfasil or Detached Madd

If Maddul Asli is in the end of a word and the next word starts with a Hamza (ء) (ا) ,then the elongation of Harooful Madd is increased to upto 3 to 5 Harakah. Such Madd is called Maddul Munfasil or Detached Madd.

Maddul Laazim or Compulsory Madd

In case of Haroof Muqataat which appears at the begining of a Sura, a Madd similar to Maddul Muttasil is applied. The length is 4 to 6 Harakah. Such Madd is called Maddul Laazim or Compulsory Madd.

Maddul Aaridh

If after Madd letters ( ی و ا ) there appears a Saakin caused by waqf or stop then such Madd is called Maddul Aaridh. The length of recitation of Maddul Aaridh will be 2 to 5 Harakah.
